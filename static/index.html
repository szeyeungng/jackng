Latest Work

Marinate (Personal)

Using the popular M(ongoDB) E(xpress) A(ngularJS) N(ode.js) technology stack, this is a web application MVP of Marinate (mobile application in the works). Users can create time capsules that are locked until the timer they set expires. While locked, users can post text, picture or video entries to their capsules that are either shared individually or with others they invited.

Office Online (Microsoft)

As a PM for Office Online, I worked on a wide spectrum of projects from customer-facing features to core infrastructure pieces to driving a data-informed culture through experimentation. The customer-facing features I shipped include Headers & Footers, Table Styles and cross-sell from Outlook.com, crafted through hours of iterative design, prototyping, user-testing and data analysis.

Warlord (Stanford ME310 + Activision)

An advanced controller for Call-of-Duty gaming on the PC in a living room setting. It enables high performance gaming through a novel doubleaiming system and an ergonomic mapping of all game functions. Pump-action reload, physical recoil and environment-based scent feedback provides high customer value by immersing users into game-play. This creates an added level of realism never before experienced in a mainstream game controller.

Pickup Artist (Stanford ME218B)

Pickup Artist was built to autonomously collect, store and dispose maximum amount of ping pong balls from the circular arena floor to either of two arena bins. It has to accomplish this uber task while simultaneously competing against another opponent and a wall, dividing the two opponents, that is free to rotate if either opponent is pushed against it.

Pongbot (Stanford ME218A)

PongBot was developed to provide all the fun and excitement of beer pong without the mess. Users partake in a fast-paced three versus three cup game, with some exciting PongBot twists. Pongbot was designed to track score, referee through closing appropriate cup holes and alert and alert users when final round begins.

Roboto (Stanford ME210)

Roboto was implemented to autonomously find, collect, transfer and shoot Peeps at targets in a controlled environment. It has to simultaneously compete against another opponent also aimed at achieving the same goal in which each opponent is on opposing sides of the arena.
